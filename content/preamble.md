🏛️ The Preamble: The Philosophy of the Indian Constitution
The Preamble is the "Identity Card" of the Constitution (as per N.A. Palkhivala). It is based on the 'Objectives Resolution' drafted and moved by Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru on December 13, 1946, and adopted by the Constituent Assembly on January 22, 1947.

📜 1. The Components of the Preamble
The Preamble reveals four major ingredients:

Source of Authority: The authority of the Constitution is derived from the People of India.

Nature of Indian State: It declares India to be a Sovereign, Socialist, Secular, Democratic, and Republican Polity.

Objectives: Justice, Liberty, Equality, and Fraternity.

Date of Adoption: November 26, 1949.

🔑 2. Detailed Breakdown of Keywords
A. Sovereign
India is neither a dependency nor a dominion of any other nation. It is an independent state. It has the power to acquire a foreign territory or cede a part of its territory in favor of a foreign state.

B. Socialist
Added by the 42nd Amendment (1976).

Indian Brand of Socialism: It is a 'Democratic Socialism' and not 'Communistic Socialism'.

Aim: To end poverty, ignorance, disease, and inequality of opportunity. It is a blend of Marxism and Gandhism, leaning heavily toward Gandhian Socialism.

C. Secular
Added by the 42nd Amendment (1976).

Unlike Western secularism (complete separation of church and state), India follows Positive Secularism.

All religions in our country (irrespective of their strength) have the same status and support from the state.

D. Democratic
Indirect Democracy: India follows a representative parliamentary democracy where the executive is responsible to the legislature for all its policies and actions.

The Preamble envisions not only political democracy but also social and economic democracy.

E. Republic
This means the head of the state is always elected directly or indirectly for a fixed period.

It also means two more things: (1) Vesting of political sovereignty in the people and not a single individual like a king; (2) The absence of any privileged class.

⚖️ 3. The Preamble as Part of the Constitution (Case Law)
The question of whether the Preamble is a part of the Constitution has been a major legal debate:

Berubari Union Case (1960): The Supreme Court stated that the Preamble shows the general purposes behind the several provisions but is NOT a part of the Constitution.

Kesavananda Bharati Case (1973): The Supreme Court rejected the earlier opinion and held that the Preamble IS a part of the Constitution. It stated that the Preamble is of extreme importance and the Constitution should be read in the light of the grand and noble vision expressed in it.

LIC of India Case (1995): The Supreme Court again held that the Preamble is an integral part of the Constitution.

🛠️ 4. Amendability of the Preamble (Article 368)
Can it be amended? Yes, as ruled in the Kesavananda Bharati case, provided the "Basic Structure" is not altered.

History: It has been amended only once so far by the 42nd Constitutional Amendment Act, 1976.

The Change: Three new words were added—Socialist, Secular, and Integrity.

🎯 5. Objectives: The Four Pillars
Justice: Secured through Fundamental Rights and Directive Principles. It includes Social (equal treatment), Economic (non-discrimination based on wealth), and Political (equal access to political offices).

Liberty: Absence of restraints on the activities of individuals and providing opportunities for the development of individual personalities.

Equality: Absence of special privileges to any section of the society.

Fraternity: A sense of brotherhood. The 42nd Amendment added the word 'Integrity' to this concept.
