# 🇮🇳 Fundamental Rights (Articles 12-35)

Fundamental Rights are enshrined in **Part III** of the Constitution. They are known as the **Magna Carta of India**. These rights are **justiciable**, meaning you can move the court if they are violated.

---

## ⚖️ The Six Fundamental Rights

### 1. Right to Equality (Articles 14–18)
* **Art. 14:** Equality before the law.
* **Art. 15:** Prohibition of discrimination on grounds of religion, race, caste, sex, or place of birth.
* **Art. 17:** **Abolition of Untouchability** (Very important for exams).

### 2. Right to Freedom (Articles 19–22)
* **Art. 19:** Guarantees 6 rights (Speech, Assembly, Association, Movement, Residence, Profession).
* **Art. 21:** Protection of Life and Personal Liberty.
* **Art. 21A:** Right to Education (added by 86th Amendment).

### 3. Right against Exploitation (Articles 23–24)
* **Art. 23:** Prohibition of human trafficking and forced labor.
* **Art. 24:** Prohibition of employment of children in factories.

### 4. Right to Freedom of Religion (Articles 25–28)
* Freedom of conscience and free profession, practice, and propagation of religion.

### 5. Cultural and Educational Rights (Articles 29–30)
* Protection of interests of minorities.

### 6. Right to Constitutional Remedies (Article 32)
* Dr. B.R. Ambedkar called this Article the **"Heart and Soul of the Constitution."**
* It allows citizens to move the Supreme Court for the enforcement of rights via **Writs** (Habeas Corpus, Mandamus, etc.).

---

## 🔑 Key Features to Remember
* **Not Absolute:** The government can impose "reasonable restrictions."
* **Suspension:** Rights (except Art. 20 & 21) can be suspended during a **National Emergency**.
* **Available to whom?** Some rights are available only to citizens (Art. 15, 16, 19, 29, 30), while others are available to foreigners too.

> **Exam Tip:** The Right to Property was removed from Fundamental Rights by the **44th Amendment Act (1978)** and is now a legal right under Article 300A.
