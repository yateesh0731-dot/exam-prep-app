# 🇮🇳 Directive Principles of State Policy (DPSP)

Directive Principles of State Policy are guidelines for the government to frame laws and policies. They aim to establish a **Welfare State** in India.

---

## ⚖️ Constitutional Provisions
* **Part:** IV (Articles 36–51)
* **Source:** Inspired by the **Irish Constitution**.
* **Nature:** **Non-justiciable** (Cannot be enforced in a court of law, unlike Fundamental Rights).
* **Purpose:** To achieve Social and Economic Democracy.

---

## 📂 Classification of DPSP

### 1. Socialist Principles (Economic Justice)
* **Art 38:** Promote the welfare of people by securing a social order.
* **Art 39A:** **Free Legal Aid** to the poor.
* **Art 41:** Right to work, education, and public assistance.
* **Art 42:** Just and humane conditions of work and **Maternity Relief**.
* **Art 43:** Living wages for workers.

### 2. Gandhian Principles (Rural & Moral Development)
* **Art 40:** Organization of **Village Panchayats**.
* **Art 43:** Promotion of cottage industries.
* **Art 46:** Promotion of educational and economic interests of SCs, STs, and other weaker sections.
* **Art 47:** Prohibition of the consumption of intoxicating drinks and drugs.
* **Art 48:** Prohibition of slaughter of cows and calves.

### 3. Liberal-Intellectual Principles (Modern Governance)
* **Art 44:** **Uniform Civil Code (UCC)** for the citizens.
* **Art 45:** Provision for early childhood care and education.
* **Art 50:** Separation of Judiciary from Executive.
* **Art 51:** Promotion of International Peace and Security.

---

## 🔄 DPSP vs. Fundamental Rights

| Feature | Fundamental Rights | DPSP |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Part** | Part III | Part IV |
| **Nature** | Justiciable (Enforceable) | Non-justiciable |
| **Focus** | Individual Rights | Welfare of Society |
| **Aim** | Political Democracy | Social & Economic Democracy |

---

## 🏛️ Legal Significance
* **Minerva Mills Case (1980):** The Supreme Court ruled that the Constitution is founded on the **bedrock of the balance** between Fundamental Rights and DPSP.
* **Implementation Examples:** * **MGNREGA** (Right to work - Art 41)
    * **Right to Education** (Art 45)
    * **Panchayati Raj Act** (Art 40)

---

> **Exam Tip:** Remember that while DPSPs are not legally enforceable, they are "fundamental in the governance of the country" (Article 37).
