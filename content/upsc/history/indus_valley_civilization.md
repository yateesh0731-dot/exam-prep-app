# 🏛️ Ancient History: Indus Valley Civilization (IVC)

The IVC was a Bronze Age civilization (3300–1300 BCE) known for its advanced urban planning.

## 📍 Major Sites & Findings
* **Harappa:** First site discovered (1921). Grainaries found.
* **Mohenjo-Daro:** "Mound of the Dead." Famous for the **Great Bath**.
* **Lothal (Gujarat):** Ancient dockyard; evidence of overseas trade.
* **Dholavira:** Unique water harnessing system and storm-water drainage.

## 🏗️ Key Features
1. **Grid System:** Streets cut each other at 90-degree angles.
2. **Drainage:** Covered drains with manholes for cleaning.
3. **Economy:** Based on agriculture (wheat, barley) and trade (seals found in Mesopotamia).

> **Exam Tip:** Note the absence of iron and horses in the early/mature phases of IVC.
