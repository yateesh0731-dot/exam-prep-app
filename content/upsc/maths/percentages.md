# 📊 Percentages: Core Concepts & Shortcuts

Percentage literally means "per hundred" (out of 100). In Business Analytics and Exams, it is the foundation for Data Interpretation (DI).

## 2. Key Formulas

### A. Basic Percentage
$$\text{Percentage} = \left( \frac{\text{Value}}{\text{Total Value}} \right) \times 100$$

### B. Percentage Increase/Decrease
$$\% \text{ Change} = \left( \frac{\text{Final} - \text{Initial}}{\text{Initial}} \right) \times 100$$

### C. Successive Percentage Change
If a value increases by $a\%$ and then by $b\%$, the net change is:
$$\text{Net Change} = \left( a + b + \frac{ab}{100} \right) \%$$
*(Note: Use negative sign for decrease/discount)*

---

## 3. The "Product Stability" Trick
**Concept:** If the price of a commodity increases, by how much should consumption decrease to keep the expenditure same?

* If Price increases by $R\%$, Decrease in Consumption = $\left( \frac{R}{100 + R} \right) \times 100 \%$
* If Price decreases by $R\%$, Increase in Consumption = $\left( \frac{R}{100 - R} \right) \times 100 \%$

**Example:** If tea price rises by 25%, reduce consumption by:
$\frac{25}{125} \times 100 = 20\%$

## 4. Exam Tips (UPSC/SSC)
1.  **"Of" means Multiplication:** 20% of 500 = $0.20 \times 500$.
2.  **Reverse Percentages:** $x\%$ of $y$ is always equal to $y\%$ of $x$.
    * Example: 16% of 50 is easier to calculate as 50% of 16 (which is 8).
3.  **Base Value:** Always identify the "Base" (the value after the word "than" or "of").
